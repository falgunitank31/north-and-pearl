# Order Readiness Orchestration Plan

Owner: Lead Orchestrator

Goal: prepare North & Pearl to start generating orders next month through aligned product, website, marketing, operations, analytics, and technical execution.

## North Star

North & Pearl should be ready for real shoppers to:

1. Find the store.
2. Trust the brand.
3. Discover relevant products.
4. Understand product details.
5. Add to cart.
6. Check out confidently.
7. Receive clear post-purchase support.

## Cross-Agent Communication Rules

- Every agent reports blockers, risks, and dependencies.
- No agent should silently change another agent's ownership area.
- If a task affects two areas, the primary owner proposes and the affected owner reviews.
- Use claim-safe language until supplier, operations, or legal facts are verified.
- Do not prioritize new content or new products over fixing a broken buying path.
- Conversion-blocking issues outrank cosmetic improvements.
- Product accuracy outranks visual polish.
- Measurement integrity outranks performance claims.

## Next-Month Order Readiness Priorities

### Priority 1: Trust and Conversion Path

- Homepage must clearly communicate North & Pearl's value.
- Navigation must make gifts and categories easy to find.
- Product pages must have accurate images, prices, descriptions, options, and add-to-cart.
- Cart and checkout path must work.
- Shipping, returns, contact, FAQ, and care pages must be clear and claim-safe.

### Priority 2: Product Readiness

- Active products must have accurate images.
- Products must be assigned to relevant collections.
- Product names must feel premium and clear.
- Product pages must avoid unsupported claims.
- Out-of-stock/source-risk products must be drafted, not left active.

### Priority 3: Traffic Generation

- Priority collections must have SEO copy and metadata.
- Gift guide content must support revenue pages.
- Internal links must move shoppers from guides to products.
- Pinterest and organic discovery assets must be prepared consistently.
- Search Console and Bing must be connected when access is available.

### Priority 4: Measurement

- Bohr must verify analytics readiness.
- Track sessions, landing pages, product views, add-to-cart, checkout starts, and orders.
- No traffic/order improvement claim is valid until data is verified.

### Priority 5: Operations

- Support email/contact path must be clear.
- Shipping and returns messaging must be finalized or labeled conservative.
- Fulfillment and supplier timing must be confirmed before stronger promises are made.
- Customer service response templates should be ready before campaigns scale.

## First Two Weeks Toward Orders

Week 1:

- Verify active catalog quality and remove/draft product risks.
- Strengthen product-page trust and image presentation.
- Publish/refresh one more high-intent buying guide.
- Prepare Pinterest organic distribution batch.
- Confirm analytics/Search Console setup gaps.
- Audit shipping, returns, contact, FAQ, and care pages.

Week 2:

- Add qualified products only where categories are thin.
- Improve best organic landing pages.
- Add internal links from guides to collections/products.
- Finalize customer support and returns workflow drafts.
- Review product/category readiness for first promotional push.
- Prepare order-readiness report with blockers.

## Escalation Rules

Escalate immediately to the Lead Orchestrator when:

- Checkout or add-to-cart fails.
- Product images are inaccurate.
- Supplier/source status becomes risky.
- Unsupported claims appear on live pages.
- Analytics cannot verify performance.
- Shipping/returns promises are unclear.
- A task requires app installs, paid spend, customer data, secrets, or irreversible changes.
