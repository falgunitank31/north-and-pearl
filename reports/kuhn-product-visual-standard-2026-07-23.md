# North & Pearl Product Visual Standard

Date: 2026-07-23  
Owner: Kuhn, Design + Brand Creative  
Scope: Product images, product-page galleries, collection cards, supplier-image acceptance, and premium jewelry art direction. No Shopify changes were made.

## Executive Standard

North & Pearl product imagery must make the product feel accurate, giftable, and premium within the first three seconds. Every published product page should show the exact item clearly, avoid supplier or competitor visual residue, and support a warm modern-jewelry experience: ivory surfaces, soft light, clean crops, readable personalization, and enough scale context to help shoppers buy with confidence.

The minimum bar is not "has images." The bar is "a customer can understand what they are buying, trust that North & Pearl is the brand, and see the piece as gift-worthy."

## First Image Requirements

The first image is the storefront promise. It appears on collection cards, search results, related products, recommendations, and often social previews, so it must pass the strictest QA gate.

- Use a clean product-first image on ivory, soft white, warm stone, or another neutral premium surface.
- Show the exact product type, motif, finish direction, and personalization area when relevant.
- Frame the product large enough to be readable at collection-card size without cropping off essential details.
- Use a square-safe composition that works at 1:1, with the primary product centered or intentionally balanced.
- Keep lighting bright, soft, and warm-neutral; avoid blue-gray casts, harsh flash, muddy shadows, or dark marketplace styling.
- Do not use model imagery as the first image unless the product itself remains the clear hero and the crop works cleanly on a square card.
- Do not use packaging as the first image unless the product is also visible and packaging inclusion has been operationally confirmed.
- Do not use text-overlay specification cards, supplier collages, comparison graphics, or foreign-language image cards as the first image.
- Do not show another brand name, logo, display card, watermark, marketplace badge, supplier SKU label, certificate, pricing, shipping badge, or claim overlay.

## Gallery Order

Use a consistent order so product pages feel curated and easy to scan.

1. Clean hero image showing the exact product.
2. Detail close-up showing pendant, charm, engraving area, stone/accent, clasp, chain, texture, or setting.
3. Scale image on neckline, wrist, hand, ear, display bust, or other realistic body/display reference.
4. Personalization or variant image when the product has letters, names, birthstone-inspired colors, sizes, lengths, finishes, or other options.
5. Styled lifestyle or gifting image that still represents the exact item accurately.
6. Packaging or gift-ready image only when packaging, pouch, insert, ribbon, or box contents are confirmed.
7. Video, motion, or 360-style asset for priority products when available and accurate.

If fewer than five images are available, the page may still pass only when the first three images are strong: hero, detail, and scale. Products with one or two usable images should remain draft until better media exists.

## Unacceptable Supplier Imagery

Supplier media must be rejected when it creates brand confusion, misrepresents the product, or weakens premium trust.

- Any image with competitor branding, supplier branding, watermarks, marketplace UI, badges, store names, QR codes, pricing, discount claims, shipping claims, or visible SKU labels.
- Product cards, jewelry display cards, boxes, certificates, care cards, or inserts from another brand.
- Foreign-language specification cards, size charts, or collages as customer-facing PDP imagery.
- Images that imply unsupported claims such as sterling silver, solid gold, gold vermeil, 18K plating, waterproof, tarnish-free, hypoallergenic, nickel-free, moissanite, natural gemstone, genuine pearl, recycled metal, or certified materials unless verified and approved.
- Collages that mix multiple products, colors, chain types, pendant shapes, stone sizes, or included accessories in a way that makes the exact purchase unclear.
- Renders, fantasy sparkle composites, AI-looking product simulations, excessive retouching, or scale-distorting close-ups that overpromise the item.
- Crops that hide the clasp, chain thickness, pendant size, personalization area, stone/accent setting, ring width, earring post, bracelet closure, or other purchase-critical detail.
- Images with hands, necklines, props, or packaging that feel low-quality, cluttered, unclean, visibly damaged, heavily filtered, or inconsistent with North & Pearl.

## Crop And Framing Rules

Product pages and collection cards must work together. A beautiful PDP crop that fails on the collection grid is not launch-ready.

- Compose primary images for square use first; keep all essential product details inside the central safe area.
- Leave breathing room around pendants, rings, bracelets, and earrings so theme cropping does not clip edges.
- Avoid extreme macro crops for the main image; use macro/detail shots later in the gallery.
- Keep necklaces oriented naturally, with pendant/charm readable and chain shape elegant rather than tangled or abruptly cut.
- Show rings with enough negative space to read the full band shape; avoid cropped arcs that make width or thickness unclear.
- Show bracelets with closure, charm, or focal detail visible; avoid flat coil crops that obscure scale.
- Show earrings as a pair or a clear single-item close-up depending on what is sold, without implying extra pieces.
- For personalized jewelry, ensure names, letters, initials, dates, coordinates, or birthstone-inspired accents are legible where they matter.
- Do not stretch, upscale, blur, sharpen aggressively, add fake depth of field, or crop supplier thumbnails into pixelated hero images.
- Preferred source size remains 1000 x 1000 px or larger; 700 x 700 px is the minimum emergency floor, not the target.

## Collection-Card Standards

Collection cards should feel like a curated jewelry case, not a supplier feed.

- First image must be collection-card approved before product activation.
- Product should occupy enough of the card to be identifiable on mobile.
- Backgrounds across a collection should feel harmonious: ivory, soft white, warm taupe, warm stone, linen, satin, or restrained display surfaces.
- Avoid visual jumps between dark supplier photos, bright marketplace cutouts, text-heavy cards, model photos, and packaging shots in the same grid.
- Maintain honest scale; do not choose a thumbnail that makes a delicate piece look heavy or oversized.
- Prefer visually quiet hero images for grid consistency, then use emotional lifestyle imagery deeper in the PDP gallery.
- Reject first images that require hover/zoom to understand the product.
- Do not publish near-duplicate thumbnails for separate products unless their titles and variants are clearly differentiated.

## Premium Jewelry Look Direction

North & Pearl should look warm, minimal, gift-focused, and quietly luxurious. The visual language should feel original to the brand, not copied from competitor layouts, ad concepts, image sequences, or styling systems.

- Palette: ivory, soft white, warm taupe, champagne-gold accents, matte black typography, and subtle neutral shadows.
- Light: bright natural light or soft studio light with gentle highlights on metal and stones/accent details.
- Surfaces: ivory sweep, warm stone, satin, linen, minimal jewelry tray, or clean display bust.
- Styling: delicate hands, polished neutral nails, simple necklines, restrained clothing, and props that support gifting or personalization without crowding the product.
- Mood: meaningful, intimate, clear, modern, and trustworthy; avoid overly dark editorial drama or bargain-marketplace brightness.
- Retouching: remove dust, glare, and distractions while preserving honest product shape, scale, finish, and texture.
- Personalization: make names, initials, dates, coordinates, and birthstone-inspired choices feel special and legible, not decorative afterthoughts.
- Gift cues: use boxes, pouches, inserts, ribbon, and message cards only after operational confirmation; until then, lean on clean product presentation and claim-safe gifting copy.

## Product-Page QA Gate

A product page is visually ready only when all of the following are true:

- The first image passes the strict first-image requirements.
- The gallery contains at least three usable images: hero, detail, and scale.
- The image order follows the North & Pearl gallery sequence or has a documented reason for deviation.
- No image contains supplier, competitor, marketplace, unsupported-claim, or unconfirmed-packaging content.
- The product is recognizable and correctly framed on desktop PDP, mobile PDP, collection card, search result, and related-product card.
- The imagery does not imply unverified material, stone, plating, allergy, durability, certification, packaging, or accessory claims.
- The visual set feels coherent with North & Pearl's premium personalized jewelry positioning.
