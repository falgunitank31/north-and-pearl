# Faraday Weekly Marketing Operating Rhythm

Owner: Faraday

Purpose: create consistent organic marketing activity that compounds into traffic, product discovery, add-to-cart activity, and orders.

## Operating Principle

North & Pearl marketing should run as a weekly loop:

1. Measure.
2. Improve.
3. Publish.
4. Distribute.
5. Feed conversion learnings back to the right agent.

Do not claim traffic, ranking, conversion, or order improvement until Bohr verifies the data source.

## Weekly Cadence

### Monday: Measure

Goal: understand what is happening before changing more things.

Faraday reviews:

- Organic sessions by landing page.
- Google Search Console impressions and clicks, when available.
- Bing Webmaster data, when available.
- Shopify collection and product views.
- Organic add-to-cart activity.
- Organic orders and revenue, when verified.
- Pinterest organic outbound clicks, when available.
- Free Shopping visibility, when available.
- Pages with traffic but weak conversion signals.

Output:

- Weekly organic status note.
- Measurement gaps for Bohr.
- Top 3 opportunities for the week.

### Tuesday: Improve Existing Pages

Goal: make current pages stronger before creating too much new content.

Faraday improves:

- Collection SEO titles and meta descriptions.
- Collection intro and bottom copy.
- Guide intros and direct-answer sections.
- Internal links from guides to collections.
- Internal links from collections to guides.
- AEO-friendly headings.
- Claim-safe product and gift positioning.

Output:

- 1-3 improved organic landing pages.
- Change log with affected URLs.
- Validation notes.

### Wednesday: Publish One New Organic Asset

Goal: add one useful, commercial-supporting content asset per week.

Allowed asset types:

- Gift guide.
- Buying guide.
- Occasion guide.
- Comparison guide.
- Care or education guide.
- AI Brand Information expansion.
- FAQ hub expansion.

Rules:

- Original North & Pearl copy only.
- No unsupported material, allergy, waterproof, tarnish, shipping, delivery, review, or sales claims.
- Every guide must link to relevant revenue collections.
- Every guide must answer real shopper questions.

Output:

- One published or publish-ready content asset.
- Internal links to commercial destinations.
- SEO title and meta description.

### Thursday: Distribute

Goal: give each content asset more paths to discovery.

Faraday prepares:

- Pinterest pin titles and descriptions.
- Organic social captions.
- Internal link placements.
- Google Merchant free-listing readiness notes.
- Product/category snippets for future email use.

Rules:

- Do not launch paid ads.
- Do not send email or SMS.
- Do not create misleading urgency or fake scarcity.
- Do not use supplier images commercially unless cleared for the intended use.

Output:

- 10 Pinterest pin drafts per week.
- 3-5 organic social captions.
- Internal linking checklist updates.

### Friday: CRO Feedback

Goal: turn marketing signals into website, catalog, operations, and measurement improvements.

Faraday routes findings:

- To Kuhn when traffic exists but visual hierarchy, UX, CTA clarity, or product-page presentation may be weak.
- To Gauss when product names, images, catalog fit, supplier facts, or merchandising block conversion.
- To Tesla when indexing, rendering, speed, schema, cart, or Shopify technical behavior needs investigation.
- To Wegener when shipping, returns, support, fulfillment, or customer-service clarity blocks confidence.
- To Bohr when measurement, attribution, event tracking, or reporting gaps prevent confident decisions.

Output:

- Weekly CRO feedback table.
- Agent-specific next actions.
- Risk and dependency notes.

## Monthly Cadence

At the end of each month, Faraday should complete:

- Top 5 organic landing page refresh.
- Search Console query review, if connected.
- Collection SEO audit.
- Product SEO sampling audit.
- Pinterest organic review.
- Internal linking audit.
- 30-day traffic and order impact review with Bohr.
- Next month content roadmap.

## Minimum Weekly Output

Each week should produce at least:

- 1 measurement/status note.
- 2 existing page improvements.
- 1 new or refreshed guide/content asset.
- 10 Pinterest pin drafts.
- 1 CRO feedback report.
